Hello, I'm April Kaye V. Muyalde

I earned my Associate's degree in Information Technology from the Informatics Computer International Institute in Baguio City, Philippines. My career as a UX designer started at Digital Arts Sales Corporation, where I was given the opportunity to enroll in a UI/UX course on Coursera. Through this course, I honed skills in creative problem-solving, empathy, and navigating complex processes to meet client objectives. Although I wasn't able to complete all the courses, I continue to stay updated on UI/UX developments through LinkedIn blogs.

To advance my career and expand my horizons, I intend to start blogging on Medium to share insights and personal experiences.

What other talents or skills should I shine a spotlight on?
My journey has been a colorful mosaic—from epic chess blunders, being a Math wizard, a dance performer, choir gigs, all while sharpening my wits in strategic thinking and creative writing. However, 2024 presents a new challenge: MASTERING CHESS, which parallels UI/UX design in requiring creative thinking. My most cherished skill, though, is my passion for drawing, which began when I was six, inspired by a first-grade classmate's artistic flair. With an architect dad and an artsy mom, drawing seemed destined, but without that fateful encounter, who knows if I'd ever have picked up a pencil?
Who are the creative geniuses I look up to?
I've got plenty to share, but the top-tier on the list are Lauren Lake, the judge from Paternity Court—she embodies the ideal blend of intelligence, compassion, and enduring grace. Watching countless episodes of Paternity Court, I've found each one imparting valuable lessons, offering hope to women like myself who seek truth, love, forgiveness, and the opportunity for redemption every day. Then there's Michael V., the Filipino comedian and a visual artist whose wit knows no bounds. And let's not forget Ding Liren, the humble chess genius whose moves I'm learning from. These individuals are true beacons of hope and talent.
What's my life's design blueprint?
My philosophy has evolved over time. I once believed, "Doubt everything, find your own light." It still holds meaning, but finding my light doesn't mean doing everything alone. Through years in the design industry, I've come to understand that "Communication is the key." It may sound simple, but expressing your thoughts is how you find your light and your people. It’s about addressing issues that bother me and empathizing with others while maintaining critical thinking. In essence, real connection sparks the brightest light.
Are there any other creative hats I'd love to wear?
Apart from my role as a UI/UX designer, I have a keen interest in animation. Bringing things to life and making them move brings me immense joy, much like creating Figma prototypes. Animation allows me to infuse movement into my creations—I live to move things. The idea of Character Design particularly in making unearthly creatures intrigues me because it unleashes my imagination without constraints or boundaries. As for logos, that's an area where I'm still honing my skills...or (Why not have someone else handle the logo creation while I take charge of the animation task?)
